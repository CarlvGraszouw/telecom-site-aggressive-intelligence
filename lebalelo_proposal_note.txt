PURPOSE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This note accompanies the browser-based presentation titled "Securing Lebalelo –
Complete IoT Perimeter Protection Powered by On-Site Sigfox Base Station." It
captures the commercial and technical rationale for deploying a Sigfox Access
Station Micro on the existing tower structure, together with Sigfox-native
sensors and cloud integration, to close a critical RF coverage gap and restore
reliable fence, perimeter, and asset monitoring.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TowerCo operates high-value infrastructure at Lebalelo where physical security
and asset protection depend on dependable IoT connectivity. Current Sigfox
network coverage at the site is insufficient: weak RF at the compound means
RAMAC™ G-Matrix and other field devices cannot consistently complete uplinks,
leading to missed alarms, delayed notifications, and reduced confidence in
automated monitoring. The constraint is coverage and link margin — not the
sensor hardware specification.

Aggressive Intelligence proposes a turnkey solution: install a Sigfox Access
Station Micro directly on the Lebalelo tower, powered from existing site
electrical and backhaul infrastructure. This delivers full local Sigfox
coverage without new civil works, with sensor traffic carried over the tower's
existing fiber or microwave link to the cloud. The result is immediate,
predictable connectivity for vibration, perimeter, environmental, and asset
tracking devices already aligned with Sigfox technology.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROBLEM STATEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

• Macro cellular and public LPWAN maps do not guarantee in-building or
  perimeter-grade service at every point on a fenced compound.

• Fence-line and ground-level sensors are often in RF shadow relative to
  distant base stations.

• Without reliable uplink, security workflows break: correlation with CCTV,
  control-room dashboards, and mobile alerting becomes inconsistent.

• Insurance and operational risk increase when electronic monitoring cannot
  be demonstrated as continuous and auditable.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROPOSED ARCHITECTURE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Tower-mounted Sigfox Access Station Micro
   – Co-located with existing power and grounding practices.
   – Uses site backhaul; no incremental recurring connectivity charge beyond
     current tower service model.

2. Local Sigfox coverage
   – Optimised for the full Lebalelo footprint, including fence lines and
     asset storage zones.

3. Sensor ecosystem
   – Perimeter: vibration and cut-detection on fence structures.
   – Intrusion: motion and thermal where required by site layout.
   – Assets: equipment and high-value movable asset trackers.
   – Environment: temperature, flood, and fire signalling as needed.
   – CCTV: event-driven triggering where integrated with the security stack.

4. Cloud and operations
   – Alarm delivery to control room systems and mobile applications.
   – Dashboards for health, battery status, and exception reporting.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
COMMERCIAL HIGHLIGHTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

• Target outcome: 100% site coverage for defined sensor classes under the new
  local cell.

• Operating expenditure: no additional OPEX line item for wide-area IoT
  connectivity beyond existing tower utilities and backhaul already in place.

• Battery life: Sigfox-native devices typically achieve multi-year (often
  10+ year) battery life where duty cycles follow vendor specifications.

• Return on investment: combined theft reduction, fewer false negatives in
  monitoring, and potential insurance benefits are modelled to support ROI
  inside nine months for the reference deployment assumptions (subject to
  TowerCo validation and local pricing).

• Scale: the same pattern—on-tower microcell plus standard sensors—applies
  across other TowerCo sites with similar coverage gaps.
